What are Molecular Orbitals?
The Foundation of Chemical Reactivity
Molecular Orbital (MO)
A quantum mechanical description of where electrons are likely to be found in a molecule. Think of it as an "electron cloud" with specific energy levels.
HOMO
Highest Occupied Molecular Orbital
The highest energy orbital that contains electrons. Represents the molecule's ability to donate electrons.
LUMO
Lowest Unoccupied Molecular Orbital
The lowest energy orbital that can accept electrons. Represents the molecule's ability to accept electrons.

üéØ Key Concepts

Energy Levels Matter

Higher HOMO energy = Easier to lose electrons = More nucleophilic

Lower LUMO energy = Easier to gain electrons = More electrophilic

The Gap is Critical

HOMO-LUMO Gap = LUMO Energy - HOMO Energy

‚Ä¢ Small gap = More reactive molecule

‚Ä¢ Large gap = More stable molecule

‚Ä¢ Optimal gap = Balance of reactivity and stability
